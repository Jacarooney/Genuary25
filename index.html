Genuary 2025

Thirty-one days, thirty-one generative artworks.

What's all this then?

Genuary is a month long happening, conceived by Piter Pasma, wherein a prompt is given each day to inspire coders to code.

Iâ€™ve decided to give it a go this year and am hosting my sketches here. Each day of January, I will (hopefully) add to the list (seen in the next box). Click on the available prompts to see and/or interact with my attempts.

I have also included some info and a link to the source code for each sketch. They were programmed using p5js, and designed to work best on a 700px square canvas... I apologise in advance for all of my barely-hanging-together spaghetti code. Desktop recommended.

  2. Layers upon layers upon layers.
  3. Exactly 42 lines of code.
  4. Black on black.
  5. Isometric Art - (No vanishing points).

  8. Draw one million of something.

  10. You can only use TAU in your code, no other number allowed.
  11. Impossible day - Try to do something that feels impossible for you to do...
  12. Subdivision.
  13. Triangles and nothing else.
  14. Pure black and white. No gray.
  15. Design a rug.
  16. Generative palette.
  17. What happens if pi=4?
  18. What does wind look like?
  19. Op Art.
  20. Generative Architecture.
  21. Create a collision detection system (no libraries allowed).
  22. Gradients only.
  23. Inspired by brutalism.
  24. Geometric art - pick either a circle, rectangle, or triangle and use only that geometric shape.
  25. One line that may or may not intersect itself
  26. Symmetry.
